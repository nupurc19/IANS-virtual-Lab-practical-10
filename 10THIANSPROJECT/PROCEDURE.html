Procedure

Encryption

In a transposition cipher, the order of the alphabets is re-arranged to obtain the cipher-text.

STEP 1 :The message is written out in rows of a fixed length, and then read out again column by column, and the columns are chosen in some scrambled order.

STEP 2 : Width of the rows and the permutation of the columns are usually defined by a keyword.

STEP 3 : For example, the word HACK is of length 4 (so the rows are of length 4), and the permutation is defined by the alphabetical order of the letters in the keyword. In this case, the order would be “3 1 2 4”.

STEP 4 : Any spare spaces are filled with nulls or left blank or placed by a character (Example: _).

STEP 5 : Finally, the message is read off in columns, in the order specified by the keyword.


Decryption

STEP 1 :To decipher it, the recipient has to work out the column lengths by dividing the message length by the key length.

STEP 2 :Then, write the message out in columns again, then re-order the columns by reforming the key word.
